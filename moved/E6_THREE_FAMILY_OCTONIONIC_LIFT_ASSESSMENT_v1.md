# Recovered E6 mechanism applied to target-free octonionic family tensors

## Question

Can the verified two-family (E_6) Higgs and seesaw mechanism supply the missing orientation-sensitive dynamics for the target-free three-family octonionic construction?

## Construction tested

For each qualified stationary octonionic vacuum, the shared positive operators (J_e,J_\nu) were lifted to canonical complex-symmetric Takagi representatives:

\[
J_f=U_fD_f^2U_f^\dagger,
\qquad
Y_f^{\rm sym}=U_fD_fU_f^T.
\]

These were assigned as the two family tensors

\[
Y_{27}=Y_e^{\rm sym},
\qquad
Y_{\overline{351}'}=Y_\nu^{\rm sym}.
\]

The recovered Eq. 83 charged-lepton operator and Eq. 84 Majorana polynomial were then evaluated with the Higgs compositions, induced triplet VEVs, and coefficients of each verified published benchmark point. No PMNS number entered this map.

The complex continuation used Hermitian canonical kinetic normalization,

\[
P=(I+XX^\dagger)^{-1/2},
\]

and the Majorana output was kept complex symmetric.

## Result

- target-free Jordan pairs: 21;
- recovered (E_6) benchmark vacua: 2;
- qualified three-family outputs: 42;
- outputs with all three PMNS angles in NuFIT 6.0 (3\sigma): 0.

Residual distribution:

\[
\min / \operatorname{median} / p_{90}
=30.73^\circ/75.04^\circ/88.43^\circ.
\]

The best diagnostic used point 2 and `primitive_minus__single_sum_phi_PPP`:

\[
(\theta_{12},\theta_{23},\theta_{13})
=(39.29^\circ,31.43^\circ,21.81^\circ),
\]

with

\[
J_{CP}=0.0640.
\]

The mechanism can generate a large complex orientation, but it does not select the observed one.

## Interpretation

The recovered (E_6) calculation is a verified two-family **fit**. Its Higgs vacuum fixes the coefficients multiplying a noncommuting family polynomial, but the two family matrices (Y_{27}) and (Y_{\overline{351}'}) remain independent fitted coordinates. The published agreement does not show that (E_6) determines their orientation.

The three-family lift confirms the separation:

1. (E_6) representation theory fixes which contractions and seesaw blocks are allowed.
2. Higgs fine tuning fixes electroweak compositions and mass-scale coefficients.
3. Neither step selects the family tensors.
4. PMNS orientation therefore remains in the family-sector input unless an additional family symmetry or target-free family potential derives it.

The recovered benchmark cannot be used as a parameter-free normalization of the earlier schematic commutator potential. Doing so would import fitted family information under an (E_6) label.

## Claim boundary

This is a structural-transfer diagnostic, not a full three-family (E_6) model. The two-family coefficients come from a published fit, the complex continuation is a declared canonical choice, and the underlying backreacted octonionic vacua lack a certified quotient Hessian because of the saturated-link second-derivative problem.

## Theory-wide consequence

The result does not affect the exact octonionic theorems or the verified reconstruction of the published (E_6) benchmark. It rules out a shortcut:

> The recovered (E_6) Higgs/seesaw coefficients do not, by themselves, convert the target-free octonionic Jordan pair into the observed PMNS orientation.

A genuine derivation now requires a principle that fixes the **family tensors themselves**. The viable routes are narrower:

- derive (Y_{27}) and (Y_{\overline{351}'}) as stationary points of a target-free family potential;
- derive them from a larger exceptional/Freudenthal state with no free family matrices;
- introduce and justify an independent family symmetry whose vacuum fixes their textures.

Without one of these, a three-family (E_6) completion accommodates PMNS but does not predict it.
