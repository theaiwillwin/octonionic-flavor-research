# Predictive flavor claim contract v1

## Objective

Promote the exceptional-geometric framework from exact representability to a predictive flavor theory.

## Non-negotiable ordering

1. Declare the field content, invariant action, coefficients, normalization and discrete choices.
2. Lock source and action hashes before computing any flavor observable.
3. Solve from generic starts.
4. Require first-order stationarity and a physical quotient Hessian.
5. Freeze one branch by an action-only rule.
6. Only then compute masses, CKM, PMNS and CP invariants.
7. Compare against matched generic-matrix baselines with the same parameter count.

## Prohibited

- PMNS, CKM or fermion masses in the action or branch selector;
- post-selection over family permutations after scoring;
- scanning an invariant coefficient and reporting only its best flavor value;
- importing fitted (Y_{27}), (Y_{\overline{351}'}), (r,s), or frame coordinates as predictions;
- calling exact reachability a dynamical derivation.

## Development-data boundary

NuFIT and historical CKM values have already been inspected in this research sequence. New comparisons are therefore exploratory development tests, not blind validation. A future dataset or separately quarantined observable is required for a genuinely blind final claim.

## Promotion criteria

A predictive claim requires one frozen action to produce:

- isolated stable vacua;
- nondegenerate charged-lepton and neutrino spectra;
- two adjacent hierarchy gaps where physically required;
- gauge-invariant CKM and PMNS matrices including CP;
- fewer physical inputs than scored observables;
- performance better than matched generic null models.

Failure at any gate localizes the missing structure and is retained as a negative result.
