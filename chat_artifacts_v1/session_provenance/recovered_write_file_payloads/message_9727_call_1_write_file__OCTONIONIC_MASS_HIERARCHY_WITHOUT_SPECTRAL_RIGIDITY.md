# Octonionic fermion hierarchies without hollow-matrix spectral rigidity

## Formal research question

> Can a three-generation fermion hierarchy be generated by a `G2`- or `E6`-covariant non-associative Yukawa operator whose vacuum breaks the exceptional symmetry spontaneously, while avoiding the hollow symmetric sign structure of the squared-associator Gram matrix? If so, can the required vacuum alignment and hierarchy parameter be selected without fitting fermion masses or CKM observables?

This formulation separates two questions that must not be conflated:

1. **Rigidity evasion:** does a modified exceptional operator escape the singular-value identity of the original hollow matrix?
2. **Physical derivation:** does independently specified dynamics select the observed hierarchy and mixing?

Passing the first question is necessary but not sufficient for the second.

## 1. Baseline operator and exact obstruction

Let

```text
u_1,u_2,u_3,H in Im(O)
```

and define

```text
M_ab = ||[u_a,u_b,H]||^2.
```

Alternativity gives `[u_a,u_a,H]=0`, so `M_aa=0`. The norm square also makes `M` real, symmetric, and entrywise non-negative. For fixed `H`, the checked `SU(3)` reduction gives

```text
M_ab = 4 (r_a r_b - |G_ab|^2),
r_a = ||z_a||^2,
G_ab = <z_a,z_b>,
```

where `z_a` is the component of `u_a` transverse to `H`, written in the compatible complex coordinates.

For every nonzero matrix in this class, weighted conditional negativity implies exactly one positive eigenvalue. Hollowness gives `tr M=0`; therefore the ordered singular values satisfy

```text
s_1 = s_2 + s_3.
```

This part is an analytic obstruction, not a numerical observation.

### Precision correction: what the obstruction does and does not imply

The sum rule does **not** force every three-generation spectrum to use the golden ratio. A normalized allowed spectrum has the general form

```text
(1,r,1-r),  1/2 <= r <= 1.
```

The golden-ratio value appears only after imposing the additional geometric-progression ansatz

```text
(1,x,x^2).
```

The sum rule then requires

```text
1 = x + x^2,
```

so `x=phi^{-1}`. Thus the correct statement is:

> The original operator forbids an exact three-rung geometric progression with any base other than `phi^{-1}`.

It is also incorrect to identify the Wolfenstein/Cabibbo parameter directly with a mass singular value. The Cabibbo parameter is a relative eigenvector angle,

```text
lambda = |(U_u^dagger U_d)_12|,
```

whereas the fermion masses are singular values of separate up- and down-sector Yukawa matrices. A complete theory must derive both spectra and their relative left-handed diagonalization.

## 2. Symmetry boundary

A fixed nonzero `H` already breaks `G2` to its stabilizer, generically `SU(3)`. Consequently, “preserve `G2`” cannot mean that the physical vacuum leaves the full group unbroken.

The viable requirement is:

> The action and operator are `G2`- or `E6`-covariant before symmetry breaking; the hierarchy arises from vacuum expectation values that break the symmetry spontaneously.

If the full exceptional group remains unbroken and the three states form one irreducible multiplet, an invariant mass operator is constrained by Schur-type arguments and cannot generically distinguish three masses. Some symmetry breaking, reducible representation structure, or additional vacuum data are unavoidable.

For the Albert algebra, the group distinction must also remain explicit:

- `Aut(J3(O)) = F4` for the real Albert algebra;
- its determinant-preserving reduced structure group is the appropriate real form of `E6`, commonly `E6(-26)`;
- a compact `E6` gauge-GUT interpretation needs a separate representation and real-form dictionary.

## 3. Recommended `G2`-covariant operator class

The original matrix is a pairwise squared distance on one generation frame. A Standard Model Yukawa matrix is instead a left-to-right bilinear map. Introduce distinct left and right octonionic generation frames

```text
u_a^L, u_b^R in Im(O)
```

and two pairs of covariant vacuum vectors `(V_1,H_1)` and `(V_2,H_2)`. Define

```text
Y_ab = c_0 <u_a^L,u_b^R>
     + c_1 <[u_a^L,V_1,H_1],u_b^R>
     + c_2 <[u_a^L,V_1,H_1],[u_b^R,V_1,H_1]>
     + i c_3 <[u_a^L,V_2,H_2],u_b^R>.
```

Here the `c_i` are real before any physical CP interpretation is assigned. The imaginary channel may equivalently arise from a complex Higgs expectation value rather than an explicit `i` in a fundamental real-field formulation.

### Why this class preserves the exceptional structure

For every `g in G2`,

```text
g[x,y,z] = [gx,gy,gz]
```

and the octonion inner product is invariant. If all matter and vacuum vectors transform together, every term in `Y` is invariant. Once the vacuum vectors acquire fixed expectation values, the physical vacuum retains only their common stabilizer.

### Why the old theorem no longer applies

The modified operator is generically:

- non-hollow, because its diagonal entries need not vanish;
- non-symmetric, because the left and right frames are distinct;
- complex when the CP-sensitive vacuum channel is active;
- full rank.

The physical masses are the singular values of `Y`. There is no trace-zero argument and no one-positive-eigenvalue theorem forcing `s_1=s_2+s_3`.

This is a minimal structural escape: it changes the operator type from a squared same-frame distance matrix to a Yukawa-like left-right bilinear while retaining the octonion associator as the non-associative ingredient.

## 5. Candidate hierarchy mechanism: sequential rank lifting

Escaping the old theorem is easy; obtaining a natural large hierarchy is the real problem. The most economical mechanism is a sequential vacuum-breaking filtration.

Let

```text
Y(epsilon) = Y^(0) + epsilon Y^(1) + epsilon^2 Y^(2),
```

with vacuum symmetry enforcing

```text
rank Y^(0) = 1,
rank(Y^(0) + epsilon Y^(1)) = 2,
rank Y(epsilon) = 3.
```

Under nondegenerate perturbation conditions, the singular values then scale as

```text
s_3 = O(1),
s_2 = O(epsilon),
s_1 = O(epsilon^2).
```

A possible octonionic symmetry chain is

```text
G2 --<H>--> SU(3)
   --<V>--> SU(2)
   --<W>--> residual discrete group or identity.
```

The primary condensate produces a leading rank-one Yukawa channel. A second vacuum direction opens one light family at first order. A third associator-sensitive misalignment opens the final family at second order and can supply an orientation-sensitive phase.

The physical analogy is ordinary crystal-field splitting: a high-symmetry multiplet begins degenerate, and successive condensates split it in stages. The octonion associator controls which splittings vanish by alternativity and which appear only after genuinely non-associative misalignment.

### Required vacuum dynamics

A target-free starting class is

```text
V_vac = sum_i lambda_i (||X_i||^2-v_i^2)^2
      + sum_{i<j} kappa_ij <X_i,X_j>^2
      + eta ||[X_1,X_2,X_3]||^2
      + xi phi(X_1,X_2,X_3),
```

where `phi(X_1,X_2,X_3)=<X_1 X_2,X_3>` is the `G2` three-form, subject to the required Hermiticity/CP completion in a physical field theory.

This potential is only a class of candidates. Unless its coefficients or discrete choices are independently fixed, it simply moves the hierarchy parameters into the vacuum sector.

## 6. `E6`-covariant alternative

Let `d(X,Y,Z)` denote the full polarization of the cubic norm on the complexified Albert algebra. For left and right states `Psi_a^L,Psi_b^R` and Higgs/vacuum elements `X_A`, define

```text
(Y_f)_ab = sum_A y_A d(Psi_a^L,Psi_b^R,X_A).
```

This is the natural exceptional-cubic analogue of a Yukawa contraction. It is generically non-hollow and is not subject to the squared-associator singular-value sum.

However, `E6` invariance fixes the representation contraction, not automatically the family hierarchy. A free family-space matrix multiplying the cubic invariant would reintroduce the entire answer by hand. A successful derivation must therefore specify:

1. matter and Higgs representations;
2. the breaking chain and light-doublet projection;
3. the generation embeddings `Psi_a^{L,R}`;
4. canonical kinetic normalization;
5. vacuum expectation values selected without fermion or CKM targets;
6. the resulting low-energy Yukawa matrices after heavy-field matching.

The `E6` route is physically richer, but it is not a shortcut around the selection problem.

## 7. Two-sector requirement and Cabibbo gate

A mass-hierarchy result is not yet a Cabibbo result. Construct separate operators

```text
Y_u = Y[u^L,u_u^R; X_u],
Y_d = Y[u^L,u_d^R; X_d].
```

Compute their singular-value decompositions

```text
Y_u = U_u Sigma_u W_u^dagger,
Y_d = U_d Sigma_d W_d^dagger,
```

and only then form

```text
V_CKM = U_u^dagger U_d.
```

The Cabibbo observable is

```text
lambda = |(V_CKM)_12|.
```

It must be held out from the vacuum-selection objective. A value close to `0.2243` is evidence only if it is obtained after all vacuum, representation, and potential data have been frozen.

## 9. Falsification rules

The program fails as a derivation if any of the following occurs:

1. the vacuum coefficients are fitted after inspecting masses or CKM;
2. a free family Yukawa matrix is introduced under an `E6` invariant contraction;
3. the hierarchy appears only in a measure-zero post-selected random frame;
4. a generic matrix with the same parameter count performs equally well;
5. the vacuum is not stationary or has unstable physical Hessian directions;
6. the mass hierarchy is produced but CKM is independently refitted;
7. “preserving `G2/E6`” is used to hide explicit symmetry-breaking spurions.

A failed gate is still useful: it identifies whether the obstruction lies in the operator, the vacuum selection, or the physical completion.
