   An Exact Spectral Obstruction to Froggatt-Nielsen Ladders
                     from the Octonion Associator

                                                   July 2026

                                                              Abstract

            In octonionic approaches to flavor, a natural mass operator on a triple of generation
        vectors u1, u2, u3  Im(O) is the associator Gram matrix Mab = [ua, ub, H]2 against a fixed
        vacuum direction H. Numerical scans of this operator found that its normalized singular
        spectrum forms a geometric ("Froggatt-Nielsen") ladder (1, x, x2) only at the golden base
        x = -1  0.618, and that Cabibbo-like bases are unreachable at any frame. We prove both
        facts. Alternativity forces M to be symmetric and hollow (zero diagonal); since its entries are
        squared norms, M is entrywise nonnegative and traceless, and the sign structure of hollow
        symmetric nonnegative matrices then yields the exact sum rule s1 = s2 + s3 for its singular
        values, at every frame and every vacuum, with no orthonormality assumption. A geometric
        ladder is therefore possible only if 1 = x+x2, whose unique positive root is the inverse golden
        ratio: the appearance of  is a theorem rather than an emergent feature, and the exclusion
        of hierarchical Cabibbo textures is exact. Numerically the pattern extends to larger frames
        as a k-bonacci family: for n-frames the sum rule s1 = i2 si holds on every sampled frame
        -- although it is not implied by matrix theory alone for n  4 -- and gradient searches
        realize the geometric ladder exactly at the reciprocal tribonacci, tetranacci, and pentanacci
        constants for n = 4, 5, 6. The program of deriving Froggatt-Nielsen hierarchies from this
        operator class is thereby closed, not merely unsuccessful.

1 Setup

Let O denote the octonions with imaginary units e1, . . . , e7 multiplying according to a fixed
orientation of the Fano plane, and let Im(O) = R7 be the imaginary subspace [1]. The associator

                                              [x, y, z] = (xy)z - x(yz)

measures the failure of associativity. Because O is an alternative algebra -- the subalgebra
generated by any two elements is associative (Artin's theorem [2]) -- the associator is a to-
tally antisymmetric trilinear function of its arguments; in particular it vanishes whenever two
arguments coincide, and for octonions it is purely imaginary.

    Octonionic and exceptional-algebraic structures (J3(O), E6) have long been proposed as
organizing frameworks for one generation of Standard Model fermions [3], with renewed recent
activity relating exceptional Jordan spectra to mass ratios and mixing [8, 9]. With the three
generations associated to a triple of directions in Im(O), a candidate flavor operator, studied
numerically in the program this note completes, is

                             Mab(U ; H) = [ ua, ub, H ] 2, a, b  {1, 2, 3}, (1)

where U = (u1, u2, u3) is a triple ("frame") of imaginary octonions and H  Im(O) is a fixed
vacuum direction (e.g. H = e7). The phenomenological target is a Froggatt-Nielsen (FN) texture
[4]: a normalized singular spectrum of the geometric form

                                            (s1, s2, s3)/s1 = (1, x, x2),

with base x = 2  0.0503 (down-type) or x = 4  0.0025 (up-type), where   0.2243 is the
Cabibbo parameter [5].

    Two robust numerical facts emerged from scans of this operator over the Stiefel manifold
St(7, 3):

   1. Direct optimization toward geometric ladders converges, with residual at machine zero, to
       the golden ladder (1, -1, -2) = (1, 0.618034, 0.381966), where  = (1 + 5)/2. Among
       8000 random frames, all 859 whose spectra were incidentally near-geometric (residual
       < 10-4) had base in [0.614, 0.622], median 0.61810.

   2. Optimization toward any other prescribed base -- including both Cabibbo targets -- stalls
       at residuals of order 10-1.

These facts were previously reported as an empirical anomaly: the operator "prefers" the golden
ratio and "refuses" the Cabibbo hierarchy. Both are consequences of a three-step argument
requiring nothing beyond alternativity and elementary matrix theory.

2 The theorem

Lemma 1 (structure of M ). For arbitrary u1, u2, u3, H  Im(O) -- no orthonormality or
genericity assumed -- the matrix Mab = [ua, ub, H]2 is symmetric, entrywise nonnegative,
and hollow (identically zero diagonal); in particular tr M = 0.

Proof. Entries are squared norms, hence nonnegative. By alternativity the associator is anti-
symmetric in its first two arguments, so [ub, ua, H] = -[ua, ub, H] and the two have equal norms:
M is symmetric. Antisymmetry likewise gives [ua, ua, H] = 0, so Maa = 0.

Lemma 2 (sum rule). Let M be a real symmetric 3 × 3 matrix with nonnegative entries and
zero trace, and let s1  s2  s3  0 be its singular values. Then

                                                     s1 = s2 + s3.

Proof. Let 1  2  3 be the eigenvalues of M ; by symmetry {si} = {|i|}. Because M is
entrywise nonnegative, the Perron-Frobenius theorem gives 1 = (M )  |i| for all i, with
1  0. Suppose 2 > 0. Then 1 + 2 + 3 = 0 gives |3| = 1 + 2 > 1 = (M ), a
contradiction. Hence 2, 3  0 and s2 + s3 = -2 - 3 = 1 = s1.

Remark 1. The sign structure in Lemma 2 is a special case of known results on hollow symmet-
ric nonnegative matrices: Charles, Farber, Johnson, and Kennedy-Shaffer [6] show that an n×n
hollow symmetric nonnegative matrix has between 2 and n - 1 nonpositive eigenvalues, which
for n = 3 forces exactly two. We include the elementary proof to keep the note self-contained;
the contribution here is the octonionic application, not the matrix fact.

Uniqueness is one-directional. Theorem 1 makes the golden spectrum the unique geometric
spectrum, not the unique spectrum: on the sum-rule surface the normalized spectra (1, r, 1 - r)
form a one-parameter family, and the original random scan realized r = s2/s1 across essentially
its full admissible range [0.5006, 0.9994]. The theorem collapses the geometric locus to a point;
it does not collapse the operator's spectral range.

Corollary 1 (exact FN obstruction). No frame and no vacuum realize a geometric ladder
at any base x = -1. In particular the Cabibbo textures are excluded exactly: for x = 2,
x + x2  0.053 = 1; for x = 4, x + x2  0.0025 = 1. More generally, any target spectrum with
s2 + s3 = s1 -- which includes every strongly hierarchical texture (1, , ) with  +  < 1 -- is
unrealizable by this operator class at any point of its domain.

    The sum rule is also the operator's positive prediction: any model in which physical mass pa-
rameters are identified (in some normalization) with the singular values of an octonion-associator
Gram matrix of this type predicts the exact linear relation m1 = m2 + m3 among the three nor-
malized values. No observed fermion mass or Yukawa hierarchy satisfies such a relation, which
is an independent phenomenological statement of the obstruction.

3 The k-bonacci family: larger frames

For an n-frame u1, . . . , un  Im(O) (n  7) the same construction gives a hollow symmetric
nonnegative n × n matrix, but for n  4 the matrix facts alone no longer force the sum rule: by
[6], hollow symmetric nonnegative matrices with only two nonpositive eigenvalues exist for every
n  4, and for such matrices s1 < i2 si fails. Whether the sum rule survives is therefore a
question about the image of the octonionic map (1), not about hollow matrices in general.

Proposition 1 (numerical). For n = 4, 5, 6, the octonion associator Gram matrix realizes the
geometric ladder (1, x, . . . , xn-1) exactly at x = 1/Tn-1, and gradient flows toward self-similarity
from random frames converge to it.

Conjecture 1 (universal sum rule). For all n  7 and all u1, . . . , un, H  Im(O), the singular
values of Mab = [ua, ub, H]2 satisfy s1 = i2 si; equivalently, the octonionic image of (1) lies
entirely in the extreme stratum (exactly one positive eigenvalue) of hollow symmetric nonnegative
matrices.

    Conjecture 1 is supported at relative precision 10-15 on 5000 frames per size but is not
implied by [6]; a proof would presumably use the G2-invariant structure of the associator (e.g.
its expression through the fundamental 3-form and its Hodge dual) rather than entrywise argu-
ments, and we leave it open. Granting it, the k-bonacci bases are the only geometric ladders
available at any frame size, and all strongly hierarchical FN textures are excluded for every n.

4 Numerical corroboration of the n = 3 theorem

The sum rule was verified to relative precision 6.5×10-16 over 2000 random orthonormal frames,
and to  10-7 over 300 frames drawn without orthonormalization and with random vacua H,
confirming that neither orthonormality nor the choice H = e7 is load-bearing. The theorem
retroactively explains all reported scan phenomenology: the tight clustering of incidentally-
geometric random frames at base 0.618 (any frame near the geometric condition is necessarily
near the golden point, the unique intersection of the constraint surface s1 = s2 + s3 with the
ladder family), the exact-zero optimization residual at base -1, and the O(10-1) floors at every
other prescribed base (the squared distance from the target ladder to the constraint surface).

5 Scope and remarks

Remark 2 (what the mechanism needs). The proof uses exactly two properties: alternativity
(antisymmetry of the associator with repeated-argument vanishing) and nonnegativity of squared
norms. It applies verbatim to the imaginary quaternions -- where it is vacuous, since asso-
ciativity makes M  0 -- and fails for generic non-alternative bilinear structures. Among the
normed division algebras, the octonions are the unique case where the operator is both nonzero
and constrained: the golden rigidity is, in this precise sense, an octonionic fingerprint.

Remark 3 (a cautionary example). The hollow structure is destroyed by seemingly innocuous
truncations. A companion machine-learning study implemented the associator keeping only the
vector (imaginary) parts of intermediate products; the truncated operator is no longer alternative,
its Gram matrix has a generic diagonal, and its self-similar spectra occur on a continuum of
bases with no golden preference. A test of the golden property run against the truncated operator
produced a spurious refutation before the discrepancy was traced. Structural claims about an
algebra must be tested with the exact algebra.

Remark 4 (n = 7: a near-miss). The case n = 7, where the frame is a full orthonormal basis
of Im(O) and the effective moduli reduce to O(7) modulo the G2 automorphism action, behaves
differently. Across 28 random starts (two barrier configurations, up to 15,000 optimizer steps)
the ladder residual stalls at  1.5 × 10-8 -- ten orders of magnitude above the machine-zero
convergence obtained with identical machinery at n = 4, 5, 6 -- with achieved bases clustering at
0.5049-0.5054, systematically about 9 × 10-4 above the hexanacci value 1/T6 = 0.5041383, and
the deviation concentrated in the two smallest rungs (s6/s1 = 0.0321 vs. 0.0326; s7/s1 = 0.0146
vs. 0.0164). The sum rule meanwhile holds to 10-16 on every solution found. We read this
as evidence that the exact geometric ladder is not realizable at n = 7. A first-order analysis
supports this: at three independently converged stall points the gradient Jc  10-8 vanishes
(true critical points, J the Jacobian of the five ladder constraints c), the Jacobian is effectively
rank-deficient (5/1  10-3 against four healthy singular values), and the residual aligns with
the weakest left-singular direction to | cos | = 1.0000 -- i.e., four of the five geometric conditions
are satisfiable and the fifth is blocked to first order, with the same residual signature at every stall
(suggesting a single canonical critical manifold). A proof of non-realizability, like Conjecture 1,
is left open.

Remark 5 (other golden-ratio appearances). Golden ratios arise in E8-adjacent contexts through
the folding of the E8 root system to H4, and famously in the E8 mass spectrum of the critical
Ising chain, where m2/m1 =  [7]. The mechanism here is unrelated and far more elementary:
no exceptional group, lattice, or integrability enters -- only alternativity, nonnegativity, and the
equation 1 = x + x2. We caution against reading deeper structure into the coincidence.

6 Conclusion

The numerical observation that the octonion associator mass operator "produces the golden
ratio instead of the Cabibbo angle" is a theorem: the operator's singular spectrum obeys the
exact sum rule s1 = s2 + s3 at every point of its domain, geometric ladders exist only at the
golden base, and Froggatt-Nielsen hierarchies are excluded not approximately but identically --
with numerical evidence that the same rigidity extends to larger frames as a k-bonacci family.
A research program seeking Cabibbo-like textures from this operator class can be closed with

                                                              5
a proof rather than abandoned for lack of results. The residual positive content is threefold: a
rigid, parameter-free spectral signature (m1 = m2 + m3) that any future octonionic-associator
mass model must exhibit or evade by changing the operator; a clean algebraic characterization --
alternativity without associativity -- of when such rigidity occurs; and Conjecture 1, whose proof
would identify precisely which stratum of hollow nonnegative matrices the octonions inhabit.

All claims are verifiable with the accompanying code: fn ladder scan.py and results (original
scans), octonion.py (exact algebra, verified on import), mechanism test.py (exact associator
