 Octonionic Flavor Geometry, Chiral Pseudoscalars,

                                                             Abstract
            We study an octonionic flavor framework in which associator mass matrices, exceptional
        Jordan observables, and Freudenthal moment maps provide a constrained geometric arena
        for Standard Model generation structure. First, fixing a vacuum direction reduces the
        octonionic associator to a complex SU (3) cross-product norm, implying a conditionally
        negative definite Gram form and the singular-value sum rule s1 = i>1 si in the nonzero
        hollow case. The full fixed-vacuum n = 7 frame problem is then constrained by tight-
        frame identities such as G2 = 2G, giving a real-algebraic obstruction pattern rather than an
        elementary topological obstruction. Second, in the J3(O) and Freudenthal setting, tested
        even geometric actions collapse to a 2 + 1 heavy-light block, while a chiral pseudoscalar
        supplies the necessary orientation-sensitive torque; its unit-generator normalization is Y =
        1/2. Third, the compact Freudenthal moment-map extraction admits a CKM-localizing tau-
        real Jordan-slot fit, but direct zero-parameter attractor closures tested here do not select
        the fitted coefficients. We therefore formulate the honest low-energy boundary: exceptional
        geometry supplies a rigid structural track and canonically normalized chiral torque, while
        an external Higgs/Yukawa wedge acts as a phenomenological selector for the fitted CKM
        spurion invariants. The result is a claim-controlled effective-field-theory framework, not a
        zero-parameter derivation of flavor.

Keywords: octonions; exceptional Jordan algebra; Freudenthal triple system; CKM matrix;
chiral pseudoscalar; effective field theory; Higgs spurion.

1 Introduction

In octonionic approaches to flavor physics, a candidate mass operator for n generations is
constructed using the octonion associator [x, y, z] = (xy)z - x(yz). For a frame of n vectors
ua  Im(O) and a fixed vacuum H  Im(O), the matrix is defined as Mab = [ua, ub, H]2. The
observed quark mixing data are encoded by the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix and the
Jarlskog invariant [1-4]; the question here is whether exceptional algebra can supply a structural
explanation of the mixing pattern.

    For n = 3, it was previously shown that alternativity forces M to be symmetric, hollow, and
entrywise non-negative, rigidly forcing the sum rule s1 = s2 + s3 and restricting the simplest
geometric ladder to the golden ratio -1. For n  4, numerical scans suggested related k-
bonacci ladder candidates through n = 6, while the full n = 7 hexanacci ladder fails. This note
separates the universally valid CND sum rule from the stronger ladder-realizability constraints,
then extends the framework to CKM mixing via the Exceptional Jordan Algebra J3(O).

2 The Universal Sum Rule via Conditional Negative Definite-

2.1 The SU (3) Reduction and Misalignment Energy

To analyze the spectral properties of M for general n, we exploit the G2 automorphism symmetry
of the octonions. By fixing the vacuum direction H = e7, the symmetry breaks to SU (3). The
7-dimensional imaginary space decomposes as Im(O)  R  C3.

    We decompose each frame vector as ua = xae7+za, where za  C3. By octonion alternativity,
the singlet part xae7 does not contribute to the associator. The associator therefore only sees
the transverse C3 parts. Direct calculation using the Cayley-Dickson convention (where right
multiplication by e7 yields e1e7 = e6, e2e7 = -e5, e3e7 = -e4) shows that the associator maps
to the complex cross product up to convention-dependent conjugations:

                                 [za, zb, e7]  2iza × zb                           (1)

Therefore, the mass matrix entries are given by the full complex norm:

                           Mab = [ua, ub, e7]2 = 4za × zb2                         (2)

Physically, this means the mass entry is a misalignment energy. If two generation vectors point
in the same complex direction (za  zb), the cross product vanishes, and no associator mass is
generated. If they span a complex area, the octonionic non-associativity stores twisting energy
in that pair.

3 The n = 7 Algebraic Obstruction

For n = 7, the frame saturates the 7-dimensional space. A natural hypothesis is that the failure
of the 7-step hexanacci ladder is caused by a topological obstruction. We show that this is not
the mechanism seen in the algebraic model.

    If the G2 structure and vacuum are transformed together, the standard quotient is:

                                                 SO(7)/G2 = RP7    (6)

The topological invariants of this space are highly restrictive. The Euler characteristic is
(RP7) = 0. Furthermore, because RP7 is one of the parallelizable projective spaces, its tangent
bundle satisfies T RP7  1 = 8. The total Stiefel-Whitney class is w(T RP7) = (1 + )8 = 1
inside H(RP7; Z2), meaning all Stiefel-Whitney classes vanish (wi = 0).

    In that moving-vacuum quotient, the five geometric ladder constraints may be viewed
schematically as a map F : RP7  R5. Because the target bundle is trivial, these elementary
characteristic classes do not force the section F -1(0) to be empty. Topology alone therefore

does not explain the observed n = 7 miss.

    The actual fixed-vacuum full-frame search is more rigid than this quotient picture: fixing

H = e7 leaves the SU (3) stabilizer of the vacuum and imposes explicit tight-frame identities on
the transverse complex columns. The obstruction below is therefore real-algebraic, not a proof

by characteristic classes.

    The true obstruction is strictly real-algebraic. When n = 7, the complex Gram matrix G

is no longer an arbitrary rank-3 matrix. Because the frame is a complete orthonormal basis of
R7, G is forced to be a scaled projection matrix satisfying the tight-frame condition:

                                   G2 = 2G, rank(G) = 3            (7)

Equivalently, in real variables, G = I - xxT + iS, with the constraints ST = -S, Sx = 0, and
S2 = xxT - I. This tight-frame condition acts as an algebraic straightjacket. It rigidly links
the diagonal lean vectors x to the off-diagonal twisting matrices S, consuming the degrees of
freedom. The system can satisfy four of the five spectral moment equations required for the
hexanacci ladder, but the rank-3 projection constraint G2 = 2G prevents the 5th moment from
vanishing. The n = 7 hexanacci ladder is forbidden by the rigidity of the rank-3 source code,
not by the topology of the ambient space.

selves define a UV-complete theory of flavor. The honest low-energy interpretation is an effective
field theory in which spacetime fields describe slow deformations of the octonionic flavor vacuum,
while a separate spurion sector supplies the observed CKM-localizing strain.

7 Conclusion

By reducing the octonion associator to a complex cross product, we have definitively closed the
spectral behavior of the mass operator, proving the universal sum rule via conditional negative
definiteness and diagnosing the n = 7 failure as a purely algebraic tight-frame saturation.

    Transitioning to the Exceptional Jordan Algebra, the verified variational searches show that
even strain energies collapse to a 2 + 1 block. The necessary mechanism found in these gates
for opening CKM-like heavy-light mixing is a chiral pseudoscalar, whose structural coefficient
is derived as 1/2. However, the direct Freudenthal attractor closures tested here do not select
the exact CKM matrix; they stall at nonzero residuals and wrong mixing angles.

    The final conclusion is that the 27-dimensional Exceptional Jordan Algebra establishes the
rigid structural track and the canonical chiral torque of the flavor vacuum, while an external
complex Two-Higgs/Yukawa spurion matrix supplies the non-linear physical strain required, in
the present framework, to deform the geometry away from its pristine baseline and lock it to the
empirical parameters of the Standard Model CKM matrix. The direct zero-parameter attractor
route tested here is closed; the structural localization framework remains as the honest output.
