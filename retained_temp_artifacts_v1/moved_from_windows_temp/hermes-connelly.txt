  Freudenthal Moment-Map Localization of CKM Mixing

Diagnostic Fits and Attractor No-Go Gates in an Exceptional Jordan Framework

                                        Matthew Connelly
                                            11 July 2026

                                                             Abstract

            We study a numerical and algebraic route for obtaining quark mixing from the com-
        pact moment map of a Freudenthal triple system over the exceptional Jordan algebra
        J3(O). Starting from two octonionic Jordan elements Ju, Jd constructed from a shared
        seven-dimensional frame, we define sector states in the Freudenthal space

                                                  = (, , X, Y )  F(J3(O))

        and project their compact generation moment maps onto a Hermitian 3 Î 3 observable. We
        verify that the scalar slots ,  are inert for the generation u(3) moment map, so scalar
        vacuum amplitudes alone cannot generate Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) mixing.
        We then show that a tau-real complex Jordan-slot ansatz,

                          Xu = Ju + rJd, Yu = Xu, Xd = sJu + Jd, Yd = Xd,

        can reproduce the observed CKM magnitudes and Jarlskog invariant to numerical precision
        for

                                             r  0.3887831255 + 0.2822870282 i,
                                             s  0.2372949892 - 0.5117538711 i.

        This is a four-real-parameter diagnostic localization, not a derivation. To test whether the
        fitted coefficients are selected by invariant dynamics, we implement the full Freudenthal
        quartic-gradient/attractor map T () = -1d() and examine self-attractor, coupled-
        plane, primitive fixed-charge, and primitive scalar-matched charge closures. All direct at-
        tractor closures tested fail to select the CKM-fitting coefficients. Thus the present work
        localizes the missing structure to complex two-Higgs-like Jordan-slot mixing, while estab-
        lishing that generic E7/F4 orbit kinematics plus direct Freudenthal attractor closure is
        insufficient for a zero-parameter CKM derivation.

Keywords: exceptional Jordan algebra; Freudenthal triple system; CKM matrix; octonions;
moment map; Jarlskog invariant; E7; attractor equation.

1 Introduction

The observed quark mixing matrix is usually represented by the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa
matrix VCKM, whose small but nonzero complex phase is measured by the Jarlskog invariant
JCP [1, 2, 3, 4]. A long-standing theoretical question is whether the CKM hierarchy and CP
phase can be derived from a deeper algebraic or geometric structure rather than fitted as free
Standard Model parameters.

    Exceptional Jordan and Freudenthal structures are natural candidates for such a program
because J3(O), the Albert algebra of 3 Î 3 Hermitian octonionic matrices, carries cubic norm,
adjoint, and exceptional symmetry data related to F4, E6, E7, and E8 constructions [5, 6, 7, 10].
The Freudenthal triple system F(J3(O)) built over J3(O) carries a symplectic form and quartic
invariant whose automorphism group is of type E7.

    This paper reports a targeted verification program around the following question:

       Can a compact Freudenthal moment map, applied to octonionic Jordan data from
       a shared frame, reproduce or derive CKM mixing?

    The answer is mixed. The moment-map observable is rigid enough to localize a highly
specific complex mixing mechanism. However, the direct invariant state-selection and attractor
equations tested here do not derive the necessary complex coefficients.

    The central result is therefore deliberately modest:

       The CKM matrix can be reproduced, to numerical precision, by complex tau-real
       mixing of two exceptional Jordan slots inside a Freudenthal state. But the tested
       invariant Freudenthal attractor closures do not select the required coefficients. The
       result is structural localization, not a zero-parameter CKM proof.

1.1 Summary of contributions

We make four concrete contributions.

  (i) We formulate the compact generation moment map for Freudenthal states  = (, , X, Y )
       and reconstruct a Hermitian 3 Î 3 generation observable H.

                                   2
  (ii) We prove and numerically verify that the scalar slots ,  are inert for the u(3)gen pro-
       jection. Hence scalar vacuum amplitudes alone cannot change CKM eigenvectors in this
       observable.

 (iii) We identify a complex tau-real Jordan-slot ansatz that reproduces CKM magnitudes and
       the Jarlskog invariant at the level of a diagnostic fit.

 (iv) We implement the full Freudenthal quartic-gradient/attractor map and show that the
       natural direct attractor closures fail to select the CKM-fitting coefficients.

1.2 Honesty boundary

The numerical fit reported below has four real fitting parameters:
                                        Re(r), Im(r), Re(s), Im(s).

It is not a prediction unless an independent invariant principle selects r, s without CKM data
in the objective. Sections 8 and 9 are included precisely to document which natural completion
attempts fail.

9.2 Attractor verdict

The full direct attractor gate is negative:

       The generic Freudenthal attractor closure, even with complex scalar slots and prim-
       itive charge spans, does not select the CKM-fitting r, s. A genuine derivation would
       require an additional physically justified boundary charge or potential.

10 Interpretation

The tested structure separates cleanly into three layers.

  (1) Orbit geometry. The E7/F4 orbit supplies kinematics and admissible state geometry.
       It is too large by itself to select r, s: dim(E7) - dim(F4) = 133 - 52 = 81.

  (2) Two-Higgs/GUT boundary data. The fitted coefficients behave as a two-sector Higgs-
       basis matrix C with nontrivial rephasing-invariant phase and singular-value ratio. This is
       where the CKM phase is localized.

  (3) Attractor dynamics. The direct Freudenthal attractor map is the natural dynamical
       selector, but the closures tested here do not choose the fitted matrix C.

    In physical terms, the result resembles the following situation. The exceptional moment-map
observable identifies the correct kind of complex "instrument tuning": the up-type and down-
type Jordan/Yukawa structures must be mixed inside the same FTS Jordan slots. However, the
instrument is not yet tuned by the generic attractor equations. A further boundary principle,
possibly from a larger GUT/Higgs potential or a constrained charge orbit, would be needed.

11 Conclusion

We have tested a concrete exceptional-algebraic path toward CKM mixing. The compact

Freudenthal moment map over J3(O) is a viable observable map, and complex tau-real Jordan-
slot mixing reproduces CKM to numerical precision. The diagnostic coefficients are

               r  0.3887831255 + 0.2822870282 i,
               s  0.2372949892 - 0.5117538711 i.

But scalar slots are inert for the generation projection, direct invariant actions do not make the
fitted point stationary, and full Freudenthal attractor closures do not select it.

    The present status is therefore:

structural localization, not zero-parameter CKM derivation.                                  (59)

The next mathematically meaningful step would be to propose a physically justified boundary
charge or Higgs/GUT potential whose invariant stationary equations select the matrix C in (29)
without CKM data in the objective.
