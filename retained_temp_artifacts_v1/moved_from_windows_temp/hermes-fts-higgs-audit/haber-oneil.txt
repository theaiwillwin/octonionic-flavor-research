Abstract
In the most general two-Higgs-doublet model (2HDM), unitary transformations between the two
Higgs ï¬پelds do not change the functional form of the Lagrangian. All physical observables of the
model must therefore be independent of such transformations (i.e., independent of the Lagrangian
basis choice for the Higgs ï¬پelds). We exhibit a set of basis-independent quantities that determine
all tree-level Higgs couplings and masses. Some examples of the basis-independent treatment of
2HDM discrete symmetries are presented. We also note that the ratio of the neutral Higgs ï¬پeld
vacuum expectation values, tan خ², is not a meaningful parameter in general, as it is basis-dependent.
Implications for the more specialized 2HDMs (e.g., the Higgs sector of the MSSM and the so-called
Type-I and Type-II 2HDMs) are explored.

I.
INTRODUCTION
The Standard Model of electroweak physics, which posits a single hypercharge-one,
SU(2)L doublet (complex) Higgs ï¬پeld, provides a extremely successful description of ob-
served electroweak phenomena. Nevertheless, there are a number of motivations to extend
the Higgs sector of this model by adding a second complex doublet Higgs ï¬پeld [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6].
Perhaps the best motivated of these extended models is the minimal supersymmetric ex-
tension of the Standard Model (MSSM) [7], which requires a second Higgs doublet (and its
supersymmetric fermionic partners) in order to preserve the cancellation of gauge anoma-
lies. The Higgs sector of the MSSM is a two-Higgs-doublet model (2HDM), which contains
two Higgs supermultiplets c
Hu and c
Hd, that are distinguished by the sign of their hyper-
charge [3, 4, 5]. This establishes an unambiguous â€œtheoreticalâ€‌ basis for the Higgs sector of
the Lagrangian. The structure of the MSSM Higgs sector is constrained by the supersym-
metry, leading to numerous relations among Higgs masses and couplings. However, due to
supersymmetry-breaking eï¬€ects, all such relations are modiï¬پed by loop-corrections, where
the eï¬€ects of supersymmetry-breaking can enter. Thus, one can describe the Higgs-sector
of the (broken) MSSM by an eï¬€ective ï¬پeld theory consisting of the most general two-Higgs-
doublet model.
In a realistic model, the Higgs-fermion couplings must be chosen with some care [8, 9]
to avoid ï¬‚avor-changing-neutral-currents (FCNC). The 2HDM can be classiï¬پed by how this
issue is addressed. In type-I models [10, 11], there exists a basis choice in which only one of
the Higgs ï¬پelds couples to the Standard Model fermions. In type-II models [11, 12], there
exists a basis choice in which one Higgs ï¬پeld couples to up-type quarks, and the other Higgs
ï¬پeld couples to down-type quarks. Type-III models [13] allow both Higgs ï¬پelds to couple
to all the Standard Model fermions; such a model is phenomenologically viable only if the
resulting FCNC-couplings are suï¬ƒciently small.
From a phenomenological bottom-up perspective, it is important to study the properties
of the most general 2HDM [14] without imposing any special relations among the tree-level
parameters. The 2HDM Lagrangian depends on two identical hypercharge-one, SU(2)L dou-
blet Higgs ï¬پelds, خ¦1 and خ¦2. If خ¦1 and خ¦2 couple identically to all other ï¬پelds (fermions and
gauge bosons), they are only distinguished by the scalar interactions contained in the scalar
Higgs potential. Consequently, one is free to choose an arbitrary basis in the Lagrangian

It is often phenomenologically desirable to express the parameters of a ï¬پeld theory as
functions of basis-independent physical observables [16, 17, 18]. This procedure has various
advantages: it leads to parameters that are uniquely deï¬پned, gauge invariant and typi-

In section II, we assumed that the scalar Higgs potential takes on the most general
possible form. However, in some two-Higgs doublet models, discrete symmetries are imposed
that constrain the structure of the Higgs potential. Typically, the discrete symmetries are
formulated with respect to a particular basis, in which case the symmetry is manifest. In

evaluated in the Higgs basis, and provide a connection to related results ï¬پrst obtained in
ref. [23].

SUMMARY
The scalar Lagrangian of the two-Higgs-doublet model (2HDM) retains the same func-
tional form under 2 أ— 2 unitary transformations among the two Higgs doublets. It is useful
to understand the eï¬€ect of these (unphysical) transformations on the Higgs potential param-
eters that govern the theory. These U(2) transformations can be thought of as generalized
rotations among diï¬€erent bases of Higgs ï¬پelds. The choice of basis is of course arbitrary.
In practice, one often chooses a basis in terms of vectors that arise naturally in the theory.
For example, in the Higgs basis, the vacuum expectation value va of the neutral Higgs ï¬پelds
points along the direction (in Higgs ï¬‚avor space) of one of the two Higgs doublets. If one
deï¬پnes Vaآ¯b â‰،vavâˆ—
b, then the Higgs basis corresponds to the orthonormal eigenvalues of
Vaآ¯b. In a CP-conserving theory, the CP-even Higgs squared-mass matrix is a 2 أ— 2 matrix
31

whose orthonormal eigenvalues can be used to deï¬پne the physical mass basis. The matrix
of squared-masses, Yaآ¯b, which appears in the Higgs Lagrangian, can also be used to deï¬پne a
basis in which the oï¬€-diagonal term Y12 = 0. When fermions are coupled to the Higgs sector,
discrete symmetries are often imposed in order to guarantee the absence of ï¬‚avor-changing
neutral currents. These discrete symmetries are deï¬پned in a particular basis, which can be
related to the structure of the Higgs-fermion interactions.
Although certain choices of basis may be physically motivated, any underlying assumption
(e.g., the existence of a discrete symmetry, CP-invariance, etc.) must be experimentally
tested. Thus, it is especially useful to analyze Higgs physics of the 2HDM independently
of the choice of basis. Starting from an arbitrary (generic) basis, one can deï¬پne invariant
quantitiesâ€”combinations of the Higgs potential parameters that are scalars with respect to
U(2) transformations in the ï¬‚avor space of the two Higgs doublets. Invariant descriptions of
discrete symmetries and CP-symmetry can be used in principle to test for these symmetries.
In addition, any physical Higgs sector observable (masses and couplings) can be expressed in
terms of the U(2)-invariants. In this paper, the relation of these observables to invariants has
been obtained under the assumption that the Higgs scalar potential is CP-invariant. In this
latter case, it is suï¬ƒcient to consider restricted O(2)-invariant quantities. In a subsequent
publication, we will generalize the analysis given in sections IV and V in order to treat the
Higgs couplings in a model with a CP-violating 2HDM scalar potential.
In the most general 2HDM, the ratio of the two vacuum expectation values, tan خ², is
an unphysical basis-dependent quantity. Typically, tan خ² acquires meaning when the Higgs-
fermion interaction is formulated, since discrete symmetries or supersymmetry must be
imposed in order to avoid ï¬‚avor changing neutral currents in conï¬‚ict with experiment. Nev-
ertheless, these symmetries are typically broken symmetries, so within the eï¬€ective theory
of Higgs interactions, tan خ² again loses its meaning. In this paper, we have advocated re-
placing tan خ² with parameters that are more directly physicalâ€”invariant combinations of
Higgs-fermion Yukawa couplings. Once again, by focusing on U(2)-invariant quantities, one
is led to a powerful and ï¬‚exible formalism that is ideally suited for general phenomenological
and theoretical studies of the two-Higgs-doublet model.
