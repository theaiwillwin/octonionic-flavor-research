## Abstract

We test a target-free exceptional-geometric route to three-generation fermion flavor. Four rank-three planes in the imaginary octonions represent independent left- and right-handed flavor frames for two sectors. With a unit vacuum direction fixed to h=e7, we construct 21 numerically independent scalar invariants from projector traces and complete contractions of the canonical G2 forms. The basis and its Haar normalization are locked before any mass or mixing observable is evaluated. We study 74 predeclared linear actions from four generic initial conditions each on Gr(3,7)^4. Sixty-eight actions reach a strict stationarity threshold and have no negative Hessian mode at the declared tolerance. Explicit reconstruction of the residual SU(3) orbit separates symmetry zero modes from physical moduli: 29 selected vacua are isolated modulo residual symmetry, while 39 possess additional flat directions. Only after these gates do we evaluate a signed associator Yukawa operator using a target-free composite vacuum direction. Of the isolated vacua, 11 select that direction and six give full-rank operators in both sectors. None has two successive decade-scale singular-value gaps. Moreover, independent O(3) gauges for the two left frames make a CKM-like matrix gauge dependent. The finite class therefore shows that stable target-free G2 vacua are readily obtained, whereas a three-rung hierarchy and physical mixing require additional structure. This is a negative computational gate for the specified invariant family, not a no-go theorem for the full G2 invariant ring.
