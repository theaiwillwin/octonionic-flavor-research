Derivation of Sterile Neutrinos in the E6

Inspired Model

In the E6 inspired supersymmetric model described in arXiv:1504.00904, the neutral sector
for each generation is expanded beyond the Standard Model (SM) to include several singlets
and vector-like states.

1. Neutral Sector Basis

The matter content of each generation is contained in the 27 representation of E6. Under
the SO(10) × U(1) subgroup, the 27 decomposes as:

                                  27 = 16(1) + 10(-2) + 1(4)

The neutral states in each generation are:
· : The light neutrino (part of the L doublet in the 16).
· c: The right-handed neutrino (SM singlet in the 16).
· s: The SO(10) singlet (SM singlet in the 1).
·  and c: Components of the vector-like lepton doublets L0 and L0c in the 10.
For a two-family benchmark, the basis is:

                          N  = (1, 2, 1  , 2  , 1  c, 2  c, s1, s2, 1  c, 2  c)T

The 10 × 10 neutral mass matrix MN has the following block structure (suppressing

3. The Sterile Neutrino Definition

In this context, the "sterile neutrinos" are the states that are singlets under the SM gauge
group:
1. Right-handed neutrinos (c): Part of the SO(10) 16-plet.
2. E6 singlets (s): The SO(10) singlet state.
These states are typically very heavy (at the seesaw scale  1011 - 1016 GeV), which
explains why the "sterile fractions" in the light neutrinos are so small ( 10-20).

4. Seesaw and Mixing

The light neutrino mass matrix m is obtained by the seesaw formula:

                                        m   -mDMR  -1mD T

where mD is the Dirac mass matrix and MR is the heavy Majorana matrix.
The sterile amplitude in the light neutrino light is given by the mixing element:

                                         Asterile  mDMR  -1
